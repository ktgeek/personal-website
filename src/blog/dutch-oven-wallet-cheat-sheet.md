As part of my preparation as a Scoutmaster for the upcoming [National Jamboree](https://jamboree.scouting.org), on behalf
of my scout leadership, I needed to get a few things laminated such as the list of tenting buddies, patrol makeup, etc.
To date, I've never owned a laminator, so this was a perfect excuse to finally break down and buy one. (As we all know,
a [laminator is the most magnificent stationery](https://youtube.com/shorts/foExoFnRMx4) (when using stationery like the
British to mean all office supplies.))

As I started on my preparations, I got distracted by a sidequest that turned into this post.

Back in 2019, when I took [Okpik](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Okpik), one of the things we got during the cooking
portions was a business card sized [dutch oven](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dutch_oven) cheat sheet to carry in one's
wallet. I've carried it in my wallet since then and have used it a ton as I love dutch oven cooking both within scouting
and outside of it. Having a charcoal temperature guide is especially important if you like to experiment and adapt
"regular" oven recipes to the dutch oven.

Unfortunately, the ravages of time caught up with it.

Looking online, I couldn't find anything similar, and Okpik was long enough in my past I forgot who made them or gave
them to us. I sat down to recreate it in Pages and Word and got aggravated with that quickly as I couldn't get either
word processor to build the design I wanted.

With my phone I took some pictures of the old card and used them when I talked to my ol' pal Claude. After some back and
forth to get exactly what I wanted, the best course of action was to create some HTML+CSS with specific instructions on
how to print the cards.

I'm super happy with my end result. I think the temperature table is a little clearer to read and all the information I
wanted to preserve is still there, but I did do some minor rewrites. I replaced the one in my wallet and handed out some
of the extras to scouts and leaders as gifts.

For those who want to make their own and share it, here is [the dutch oven card html
file](/assets/files/dutch_oven_cards.html) that you can print and use for yourself. Feel free to make your own copy and
share it with others too. The point of this blog post was to give the file a permanent home for others like me who may
have been searching for something like this.
